3R

Reducing focuses on minimizing waste by making thoughtful choices in consumption. This includes selecting products with minimal packaging, avoiding single-use items, and prioritizing essentials over excess. By reducing waste at its source, natural resources are conserved, energy usage is lowered, and the strain on landfills is significantly reduced.

Reusing emphasizes giving items a second life through repurposing or extended use. Old jars can become storage containers, clothing and furniture can be donated, and reusable bags, bottles, or utensils can replace disposable ones. This practice reduces the demand for new products, preserves resources, and contributes to sustainability by maximizing the value of materials.

Recycling transforms waste materials into new products, reducing the need for raw resources and preventing landfill overflow. Materials such as paper, plastic, metal, and glass are processed into new items, conserving energy and lowering greenhouse gas emissions. Recycling promotes environmental protection and supports the creation of a circular economy.

Do and Don'ts

  1. Do Sort Properly

    2. Ensure items are placed in the correct bin (e.g., paper in the blue bin, glass in the brown bin, and plastics/metals in the orange bin).

  2. Do Rinse Containers

    3. Clean food residue from containers like jars, cans, and bottles before recycling.

  3. Do Flatten Boxes

    4. Break down cardboard boxes to save space in the bin.

  4. Do Use Designated Bags

    5. If required, use clear or specific recycling bags to store materials.

  5. Do Check Labels

    6. Follow local recycling guidelines and bin labels to avoid contamination.

  6. Do Include Dry Items

    7. Make sure recyclables are dry to prevent mold or damage.

  7. Do Recycle Electronics Responsibly

    8. Take e-waste to designated drop-off points instead of putting them in standard recycling bins.

  1. Don’t Contaminate

    2. Avoid placing non-recyclable items like food waste, greasy pizza boxes, or disposable diapers in recycling bins.

  2. Don’t Bag Recyclables (Unless Required)

    3. Loose items are better for sorting, so avoid bagging unless specified by your local program.

  3. Don’t Include Hazardous Waste

    4. Batteries, chemicals, and medical waste should never go in standard recycling bins.

  4. Don’t Recycle Plastic Bags

    5. These should be taken to special collection points, not placed in curbside bins.

  5. Don’t Overfill the Bin

    6. Ensure the lid can close properly to prevent windblown litter.

  6. Don’t Include Sharp Objects

    7. Items like broken glass or needles should be disposed of through proper waste channels, not recycling bins.

  7. Don’t Assume Everything is Recyclable

    8. Always double-check local guidelines before recycling unfamiliar items.
